It really seems like Ivy was specifically made in a lab just for me... I'm still boggled by it. As of revamping this, Engage is a year old and I have not been able to think about anything else since that fateful release date. The last time I went this insane was over some extremely obscure cartoon no one has ever heard of a decade ago. I routinely forget that Ivy is a canon character and not some id -pandering OC I made for myself. Which is obviously impossible; If she was an OC, there wouldn't be half as many images of her and any compiled information wouldn't be posted in public. (That's a self drag.)

During the pre-release, I thought Engage's art style was "not my thing" but I was willing to give it a chance anyway. But even back then, the only characters that stuck out to me design-wise was Alear and Ivy's despite writing her off. I was in the camp who thought Ivy was going to be a rehashed Camilla or Loki (EW!!) and wasn't charmed by the pre-release trailer. I still thought her official art was lovely even though the portrait had an "off" vibe... It looked OOC from the "evil and horny" (sorry) assumption I had. (I thought the fascinator was weird too, but I didn't know those actually existed like with Lissa's crinoline...) Hindsight is 20/20, because this was probably an intentional clue on her real personality. I think it's clever and likely on purpose that she's purple and the outlier when it comes to Elusia's faith... What does red and blue make? That's what I thought. Even the alt in FEH nods to this too. I don't think this is spitballing when it turned out there's in-story reasons for Alear's red-and-blue palette. It proves my point how Engage is not deep but it still has some nice tidbits of depth if you look for it. The rose spikes are kind of goofy, but I got used to them. It looks like Lucario ! Autistic people LOVE Lucario !

One year later , I think the ghost quirk has been flanderized too much by players. It's not even brought up in the main story... (Did you know the Ally Notebook entry was originally about Ivy's skin instead of ghosts? Treehouse is back at it again...) But I can't deny that was the hook that sold me onto her... It's such an endearing (borderline childish) quirk for someone otherwise so serious and mature. Her supports with Timerra and Panette made me lose my shit!!! I'm a huge fan of when characters have an unexpected weakness and react to it disproportionately, I'm fueled by what's funny to me above all. Great motivator to stay unspoilered in the future, since I went into Engage 95% blind. But to be honest, I had a feeling I would be in deep the moment I heard her voice. I've been a "sub snob" for 20+ years, and a Treehouse-skeptic for 7, yet... When I watched clips of the Japanese audio, I was disappointed. I don't think Yōko Hikasa is bad at all, but its also not nearly as charming to me as Reba Buhr's Ivy!!! Who I find out also voiced Jill, but I never pulled her in Heroes...

I've mentioned several times that I like how Engage is lighter compared to Three Houses, but I wish its serious moments were expanded upon or more frequent. Ivy and several other characters (Hortensia, Saphir, Alfred, Yunaka, etc.) would've benefitted from being in a more serious game. Ivy's childhood is something I wish there were more details on than "it was bad" ... It makes me sad to think about how she spent so long living in fear and hiding, with no trustworthy adults in her life. It's even telling how she has nicer things to say about Hortensia's now-dead mother than her own. I'm more compelled by how Three Houses handled the topic of faith , but I do think it's sweet how her faith in Alear out of rebellion was what kept her going. It's also kind of cute how something so "wholesome" is rebellious for her... Out of all the token "characters that are not-subtly in love with the protagonist" in nu-Fire Emblem, I think Ivy has the most understandable motive. Shout out to Tharja stalking Robin because she was horny . I think Ivy's devotion to Alear is the other thing players have flanderized too much, but it's probably the only (or few) instances of literal "avatar worship" that makes sense. Not sorry, but Edelgard's attachment to Byleth makes no sense in non-Crimson Flower routes.

The go-to positive opinion is that her design is sexy (either just that or "done correctly/not over-the-top" depending who says it), but I personally also find it cute... I just try not to think too hard about how over-decorated the main outfit is and what's going on with that cape... The fascinator covers her face too much in cutscenes and supports, which is almost a shame because I think her expressions and eyes during wake up events are adorable. I can't put into words how much I regret giving her food she disliked... Engage runs on Unity instead of Koei Tecmo's engine used in Three Houses, so there's a lot of awkwardness with the models like the clipping and camera angles (everyone's hands look big because it's just angled in front of their face...). My nitpicks haven't deterred me from spending an entire lunch break staring at her anyway. I've seen a lot of people say the casual wear is weird because it looks "too modern", but I don't mind because it 1. makes fanart easier, 2. it's an interesting peek at everyone's personalities to show what they wear outside of their armor/class outfits. I unfortunately didn't even notice there are blurbs about each outfit until my second playthrough, and I was immediately endeared to the fact that Ivy made her dress! (And that Hortensia's casual wear is based off of Ivy's Wing Tamer outfit... Awww.) Even her wyvern has personal touches!!!

I started reading the manga in February 2024 , since Ivy has appeared in Chapter 10 and beyond. I consider the manga to be more of an interpretation of the game approved by Intsys than hard canon, but I'm already enjoying the major changes like Lumera not dying so quickly. And of course I'm also in love with how Ivy is drawn in it... I think it captures her serious and melancholy side really well. I've tried to draw Ivy several times and haven't been finished most attempts, but it's unexpectedly very close to my vision of her! As you can see, Kyou Kazuro is prone to having heated horny moments in several panels (we all saw it coming with how he drew Lumera and Chloe), but it's published in Shounen Jump. You know how it is...

I don't want to get too off topic and talk about other games, but ... (oh who cares, it's my page) I lost the save files for my attempted playthroughs of the Archanea games, but I like Minerva's archetype and what I saw of her in Heroes and Warriors. I know for a fact she would be one of my favorites if I finished those games. (I mean, I like Melady who is 80% identical to her...) It was obvious from day one that Ivy is based on Camilla, which is another point in her favor...but maybe not in the way you'd think. Camilla was one of the many Fates characters I always thought had wasted potential and nearly all of her alts in Heroes make me wretch. Possible unpopular opinion, but my idea of "fixing Camilla" is not downplaying her flaws and playing up her positive traits (which Engage's DLC tried to do...); I just wish Fates was more self-aware that her behavior is a product of her environment instead of being coomer about it. Maybe one day I'll replay Fates and post a full hot take about it.

When it comes to polarizing characters in nu-Fire Emblem, I'm only marginally more sympathetic to Camilla compared to Tharja because she has a Freudian excuse for being like that. But even, it wasn't enough to take away the fact that Fates was a trainwreck in several ways. It's not THAT bad, but it's not that good either. Good thing it's easy to mod your 3DS now and just grab a .cia of the special edition. There isn't anything wrong with characters who are "problematic" or bad people, for the record. I just had the misfortune of my only Fire Emblem fandom experience happen on Tumblr for 5 years before Three Houses made me snap, and I got tired of the trend of guilt tripping people to "rehabilitate" badly handled female characters as diet social justice. You can preach that characters like Camilla's writing is informed by misogyny all you want, but at the end of the day it's perfectly understandable to not like them for that same reason. It's not my job to "fix" things I don't already like, if canon sucks I hit the bricks. That's what I mean when I say that Ivy is "the good version" of Camilla ; It's not in a moral sense (just look at Fell Ivy), but Ivy has the potential I liked about Camilla without the baggage of being in three incomplete games sold separately. If you want to get fake deep about it, I like that they both had a similar upbringings but came out of it in opposite ways.

More reasons why

...Are you still here? Wow... I initially kept more personal feelings private, but I wanted to wait so this wouldn't turn out to be a weird phase I feel silly about later. (If it does, I'll keep this page up and leave a disclaimer about it.) ANYWAY... Engage dropped immediately after I exited one of the most traumatic times of my adult life (2018-2022), and I was able to enjoy things again. One thing that happened during then was I became completely anhedonic to my hobbies due to psychiatric issues. I immediately regretted holding back my enjoyment of things out of fear it made me look dumb, because I thought I would never feel that way again. On older versions of other pages I mentioned how I "wished" I was into the whole self-insert thing, mainly because I wanted to be able to feel that kind of passion. Three Houses was released during that time and was a huge comfort game at the time for me, but the association I have of the year it was released and the early game slog is why I don't consider it a top favorite game anymore.

2023 was really rough for me, my job at the time put me at my absolute limit and I was having a hard time finding local friends and community. I had a lot of internalized lesbophobia and felt like I was incapable of having a fulfilling life. I bought Engage because it was released on my paycheck, and playing it on breaks at work was a big highlight of my year. A lot of this started out as an elaborate bit or "favorite character" type deal (the early stages of this page was a high-effort shitpost), but it stopped being ironic by March and then became completely serious by July. I initially hesitated because I thought it made me look like a weirdo (as if I didn't previously?) or hugblanketer (not true, I'm now dating someone who doesn't mind and my feelings didn't change); But I couldn't help how I feel. I've always struggled with emotional regulation after getting off medication and it's led to saying and doing things I regret. I've reacted immaturely to situations and said inappropriate things that hurt people I cared about. The push to accepting it was that if this isn't hurting me or anyone else, it's not a problem at all. ("Our" "anniversary" is 7/7, I chose the date of her Heroes appearance because it was a funny coincidence.)

Autism. Case closed.
